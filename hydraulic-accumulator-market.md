<p><p>Hydraulic accumulators are classified into different types based on their design and functionality. The four main types are bladder, piston, diaphragm, and other types. Bladder accumulators utilize a flexible bladder to store hydraulic fluid under pressure, while piston accumulators use a movable piston to store energy. Diaphragm accumulators rely on a flexible diaphragm to separate hydraulic fluid and gas. Other types of accumulators may include combination designs or innovative technologies for specific applications. Each type offers unique advantages and is suitable for various hydraulic systems.</p></p>
