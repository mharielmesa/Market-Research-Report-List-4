<p><p>Electric DC motors are divided into two main market types: brushed and brushless. Brushed motors utilize brushes to conduct electrical current within the motor, resulting in wear and tear over time. Brushless motors, on the other hand, do not require brushes, leading to improved efficiency and longevity. Brushless motors are generally more expensive but offer higher performance levels. Both types of motors have their own set of applications and advantages, catering to different market needs and requirements.</p></p>
