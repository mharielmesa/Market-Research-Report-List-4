<p><p>Cervical retractors are medical devices used during surgeries to hold tissues and organs aside, providing better visualization of the surgical area. There are two main types of cervical retractors: transverse retractors, which are placed perpendicular to the surgical site, and longitudinal retractors, which are placed parallel to the surgical site. Transverse retractors are used for wider exposure, while longitudinal retractors are used for deeper and more precise visualization of the surgical field. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring successful outcomes in cervical surgeries.</p></p>
