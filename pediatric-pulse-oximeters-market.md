<p><p>Pediatric pulse oximeters are medical devices used to measure oxygen levels in children. There are two main types of sensors used in pediatric pulse oximeters: wrap sensors and clip sensors. </p><p>Wrap sensors are designed to be wrapped around a child's finger, toe, or wrist, providing continuous oxygen saturation readings. Clip sensors, on the other hand, are attached to a child's earlobe or finger using a clip mechanism. Both types of sensors are essential for accurately monitoring oxygen levels in pediatric patients and ensuring timely medical interventions when needed.</p></p>
