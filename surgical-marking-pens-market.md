Single ink pens contain just one color of ink, usually black, and are simpler in design. Both types of pens are commonly used in surgical settings to mark incision sites, identify anatomical structures, and communicate important information during procedures. The choice between dual ink and single ink pens depends on the specific needs of the surgeon and surgical team.</p></p>
