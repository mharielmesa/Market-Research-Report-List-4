<p><ul><li>Single Factor Authentication</li><li>Two Factor Authentication</li></ul></p>
<p><p>User authentication solutions in the market can be classified into Single Factor Authentication and Two Factor Authentication. Single Factor Authentication involves the use of a single form of verification, such as a password or a fingerprint. Two Factor Authentication, on the other hand, requires two different forms of verification, typically something the user knows (like a password) and something the user has (like a physical token or a mobile device). Both solutions aim to provide secure access to user accounts and protect against unauthorized access.</p></p>
