<p><p>A cardiac stent is a small mesh tube utilized to treat narrowed or blocked coronary arteries, thereby improving blood flow to the heart. These devices are often used in conjunction with angioplasty, a procedure that opens up these arteries. The stent remains in place permanently, providing structural support to the artery and preventing its re-narrowing.</p><p>The cardiac stent market is witnessing significant growth driven by factors such as an increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, advancements in stent technology, and rising geriatric populations. Moreover, the growing awareness regarding preventive healthcare and developments in minimally invasive procedures contribute to market expansion. </p><p>Latest trends in the cardiac stent market include the emergence of bioresorbable stents, which offer the benefit of temporary support while being absorbed by the body over time, reducing the risk of long-term complications. Additionally, advancements in drug-eluting stents enhance effectiveness, targeting specific health conditions and improving patient outcomes. </p><p>Overall, the Cardiac Stent Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.21% during the forecast period, reflecting a robust demand for innovative cardiac interventions and improved treatment options.</p></p>

<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Cardiac Stent Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bare-metal Stent (BMS)</li><li>Drug-eluting Stent (DES)</li><li>Bioresorbable Vascular Scaffold (BVS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cardiac stent market comprises three main types: Bare-metal Stents (BMS), which are simple devices providing temporary support to blood vessels; Drug-eluting Stents (DES), which are coated with medication to prevent restenosis and improve long-term outcomes; and Bioresorbable Vascular Scaffolds (BVS), which gradually dissolve in the body, allowing natural vessel function over time. Each type serves specific patient needs, with BMS offering a straightforward option, DES enhancing treatment efficacy, and BVS representing innovative, biocompatible advancements.</p></p>
