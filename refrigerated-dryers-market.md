<p><p>Refrigerated dryers are commonly categorized into two types: Intermittent Type and Continuous Type. Intermittent Type refrigerated dryers operate based on demand, cycling on and off to maintain a desired level of air dryness. On the other hand, Continuous Type refrigerated dryers run constantly to ensure a continuous supply of dry air. Each type caters to different needs and preferences, with Intermittent Type being more energy-efficient and cost-effective for applications with varying air demand, while Continuous Type is suitable for continuous and consistent air drying requirements.</p></p>
