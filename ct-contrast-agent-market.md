<p><p>CT Contrast Agents are used in imaging scans to enhance visibility of internal body structures. There are two main types of contrast agents: Iodine-based and Barium Sulfate compounds. Iodine-based agents are commonly used for vascular imaging while Barium Sulfate compounds are used for gastrointestinal imaging. Iodine-based agents are water-soluble and provide clearer images of blood vessels, organs, and tissues. Barium Sulfate compounds are insoluble and provide better visualization of the digestive system. Both types play a crucial role in improving diagnostic accuracy in medical imaging.</p></p>
