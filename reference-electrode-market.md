<p><ul><li>Aqueous Reference Electrode</li><li>Nonaqueous Reference Electrode</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The reference electrode market comprises two primary types: aqueous and nonaqueous reference electrodes. Aqueous reference electrodes operate in electrolyte solutions, commonly used in electrochemical measurements due to their stable potential and accessibility. In contrast, nonaqueous reference electrodes function in organic solvents or non-aqueous environments, suited for specialized applications, such as those in battery technology or specific chemical reactions. The choice between these types depends on the specific requirements of the electrochemical system, including stability, compatibility, and application context.</p></p>
