<p><p>Underfloor heating for residential buildings refers to a method of heating where heat is emitted from under the floor, providing a more even and efficient distribution of warmth throughout the space. There are two main types of underfloor heating systems - hydronic heating and electric heating. Hydronic heating uses hot water circulated through pipes under the floor, while electric heating uses electric cables or mats to generate heat. Both types are becoming increasingly popular in residential buildings due to their energy efficiency and comfort.</p></p>
