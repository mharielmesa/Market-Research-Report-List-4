<p><p>Primary switchgear refers to the main power distribution equipment that receives power from the transmission network and distributes it to various substation feeders. Secondary switchgear, on the other hand, is responsible for further distributing power to individual loads within a facility or building. The primary switchgear market is focused on larger-scale distribution networks, while the secondary switchgear market caters to smaller-scale distribution needs within specific premises. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring efficient and safe power distribution.</p></p>
