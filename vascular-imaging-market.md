<p><ul><li>Magnetic Resonance Imaging</li><li>Computed Tomography</li><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Nuclear Imaging</li><li>X-ray</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Vascular imaging market offers various types of technologies to visualize blood vessels and diagnose vascular diseases. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) uses magnetic fields and radio waves to create detailed images of blood vessels. Computed Tomography (CT) scans use X-rays to produce cross-sectional images of blood vessels. Ultrasound uses sound waves to create images of blood flow. Nuclear Imaging involves injecting a small amount of radioactive material to create images of blood circulation. X-ray imaging uses high-energy electromagnetic waves to capture images of blood vessels.</p></p>
